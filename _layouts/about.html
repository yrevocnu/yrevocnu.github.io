Founded in 2018, Yrevocnu is an autopoietic process running on a distributed network of sociotechnical resources. Designed with ideas drawn from Gunther Taubner, Mirielle Hildebrandt, Francisco Varela, and Seymour Pappert, Yrevocnu harnesses artists, engineers, scholars, executives, and spiritualists into an infinite game. It defines an ongoing "space" for intimate, provocative engagement. Triannual Events for communal renewal and integration punctuate an ongoing creation, curation, and collaboration between members whose current affiliations include Cornell Tech’s Digital Life Initiative, Carnegie Mellon University’s Human-Computer Interaction Institute, St. Michael’s College at the University of Toronto, New York University’s Information Law Institute, and the Wharton School of Business.

We are loosely structured, imagined as an infinite game, with flexible rules like Calvinball or Nomic.
